County Begins Work for 2017 Revaluation.

Tyrrell County like all North Carolina counties is mandated by state law to place a market value on all taxable real estate at least once every eight years. Tyrrell County has recently begun work on the state mandated 2017 revaluation. The county tax office will be assisted by Pearson’s Appraisal Services, Inc. in reviewing all taxable county real property.

Preliminary appraisal work will begin in December and continue throughout 2016. Appraisers will be in the county reviewing real estate and measuring and listing buildings and improvements as necessary. Current tax office records along with recent market value sales will be the corner stone of the revaluation.

Market value is what a willing buyer will pay a seller when the seller is not forced to sale. Arm’s length sales are the best indicator of what property is currently worth throughout the county. The tax office relies on the free market of buying and selling real estate to set the market value.

Appraisers will be visiting properties throughout the county and the appraisers will have identification cards or badges. If the owner is not at home when the appraisers visit, a door hanger will be left with general information and local contact numbers for questions.

As the project progresses the tax office will make more information available to the public. The notices of new value for 2017 will be mailed in late 2016. Property owners will have ample opportunity to meet with appraisers after the new values are assigned to discuss or appeal their new value. Tyrrell County is committed to citizen involvement for the most equitable revaluation result for 2017.
